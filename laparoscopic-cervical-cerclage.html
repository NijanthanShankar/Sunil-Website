Laparoscopic Cervical Cerclage Treatment in Bangalore

Laparoscopic cervical cerclage is a surgical procedure performed to prevent premature birth or miscarriage in women who have a weakened or incompetent cervix. The cervix, which is the lower part of the uterus, sometimes weakens and opens too early during pregnancy, which can result in premature delivery. Cervical cerclage helps to strengthen and close the cervix to support the pregnancy.

In a laparoscopic cervical cerclage, the procedure is done using minimally invasive techniques. Instead of making a large incision, the surgeon uses small incisions and a camera (laparoscope) to place a stitch around the cervix. This stitch holds the cervix closed, helping to maintain the pregnancy until the baby reaches a safer term.

Indications

  • Women with repeated 2nd trimester miscarriages with no known cause.
  • Women with history of uterine abonormility
  • Women with a history of previous cervical surgery or trauma that may have weakened the cervix.
  • Women with an abnormal cervix, such as a cone biopsy or a trachelectomy.
  • Women with a short cervix, which is a risk factor for premature birth.
  • Women is failed trans vaginal cerclage

Procedure

It is performed under general anesthesia. The procedure involves the following steps:

  • Co2 is used to inflate the abdomen to ease the introduction of instruments into abdomen and avoiding injury to neighbouring organs.
  • A small incision in the belly button and a laparoscope, which is a thin, lighted tube with a camera attached to the end is put through which allows the surgeon to visualize the cervix and surrounding structures. 2 other small cuts are needed to facilitate instruments and to perform the surgery.
  • A synthetic tape is passed through the tunnel and around the cervix at the level of internal opening of cervix, after separating bladder from Cervix. The suture is tightened to hold the cervix closed during pregnancy.
  • The instruments are then removed and the cuts are closed with sutures or surgical glue.
  • The patient is then monitored in the recovery room and may be discharged the same day or kept overnight for observation.
Who should perform the surgery

It should be performed by a qualified well experienced laparoscopic surgeon who has received higher level specialized training in this procedure. The surgeon should have expertise and knowledge in both laparoscopic surgery and cervical encerclage. The surgeon should have a good understanding of the anatomy and physiology of the female reproductive system.

Discussion should include the surgeon's experience and qualifications, potential risks and benefits of the procedure and also about the success rates.

The patient and couple should choose a surgeon with good expertise and who has a track record of successful outcomes with laparoscopic abdominal cervical cerclage .

The woman should receive personalized care throughout the treatment process. Patients should consider factors such as the surgeon's training, experience, and patient reviews of the team.
