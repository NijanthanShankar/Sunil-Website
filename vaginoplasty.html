How it is performed
  • It is done under general anesthesia, which means the person will be asleep during the surgery.
  • The operating surgeon will make several small 0.5cm cuts in the abdomen, through which the laparoscopic instruments will be introduced.
    How to create a new vagina
    Peritoneal Grafting: One common method involves using a segment of the peritoneum (the membrane lining the abdominal cavity) to create the vaginal canal.
    The surgeon carefully dissects a segment of the peritoneum, typically located near the rectum, and then brings it down through the pelvis to the vaginal introitus (the vaginal opening).
    The graft is positioned and secured to form a tubular structure, creating the neovaginal canal.
    The newly created vaginal canal is positioned and secured into place, typically with sutures, and connected to the vaginal opening.
  • In the recovery period, the individual will have to use dilators to keep the vaginal canal open and prevent it from closing back. This is done several times a day for several months after the surgery.
  • The person will need to stay in the hospital for a 2 to 3 days .
  • Post surgery one will need to avoid strenuous activity and sexual activity for several weeks or months, depending on their individual recovery time.

Disadvantages of surgery

Laparoscopic vaginoplasty is a technically complex demanding procedure that requires specialized training and experience. Surgeons who are not experienced in this technique may not achieve optimal results, which can result in complications or the need for additional surgery.

It is a relatively new surgical technique and may not be available at all healthcare facilities.

As with any surgical procedure, there is a risk of complications with laparoscopic vaginoplasty. These can include bleeding, infection, nerve damage, or problems with healing.
