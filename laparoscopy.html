What happens in laparoscopic surgery?

Instead of creating an outsized incision through the skin (laparotomy, or open surgery), a laparoscopic surgeon makes two or three small incisions to perform the surgery. These 0.5-1-cm incisions function entry ports for an extended, narrow camera and other laparoscopic instruments. The high-definition camera shows a exaggerated read of the pelvic organs to the surgeon. Specially designed instruments change the surgeon to perform all the fragile manoeuvres to get rid of diseased tissue exactly, have a meticulous management of bleeding, and even perform fine tissue suturing.

Laparascopy procedure is much more effective surgical technique compared to Open pelvic surgeries.

The benefits of laparoscopic surgery:

Since laparoscopic surgery causes less trauma to the body, recovery is far quicker than with ancient open surgery. Because the incisions are smaller, pain is considerably reduced with less need for pain medication after surgery. Patients will usually get up within a few hours of surgery.

Hospital stay is reduced to 1-3 days, compared to up to a week following open surgery. Several procedures are often even done as single-day surgery. This means recovery within the comfort of your own house and having the ability to resume employment within a few days.

In addition to the better cosmetic healing of tiny incisions, laparoscopic surgery additionally reduces the danger of infection, as a result of minimal tissue handling compared to open surgery.

In summary the benefits of laparoscopic surgery are:

  • less pain
  • less trauma and less invasive
  • less scarring
  • shorter hospital stay
  • quicker recovery and return to normal activity

What are the risk?

All types of surgery carry risks. Indeed, it might be impractical to expect any surgery to be completely riskless. Generally, laparoscopic surgery is considered safe and its associated risks are uncommon. Most of those risks happen at a similar rate, if not lower, as in traditional open surgery

Side effects because of anesthesia (temporary nausea, vomiting) Infection - E.g. fever after operation, urinary tract infection, wound infection. The overwhelming majority of infection issues may be effectively treated with antibiotics.

Serious complications after the laparoscopy are rare. it's calculable that they happen in one out of each 1,000 cases. Serious complications include:

Injury to near by organs like bowel, bladder or ureter.These injuries are typically fixed with surgery. Other risk post surgery could be blood clot developing in a vein (deep vein thrombosis - DVT). Early mobilization post laparoscopic surgery is a technique to scale back the chance of post-operative DVT.

Other factors like patient’s age, general health, the rationale for surgery, previous pelvic or abdominal surgery, previous Caesarean sections as well as the complexities of laparoscopic surgery also contribute to the particular risk of an individual patient.

During your personal consultation, our doctor can speak deeply about the risks in specific to your individual condition.

What to expect after a laparoscopic surgery?

Recovery following surgery depends on the sort of procedure you've undergone. In general, it is common to experience the following symptoms for 1-2 days:

Pain at the site of incision – Adequate pain medication are going to be provided. Shoulder tip pain - Laparoscopic surgery involves inflating the abdomen with carbon dioxide gas. The residual gas may irritate the diaphragm, resulting in referred pain at the shoulder tips. This pain can resolve in 1-2 days because the gas is naturally absorbed by the body.

Mild nausea after anaesthesia

Mild bloating sensation over stomach – because of the residual carbon dioxide gas.

Minor bleeding and bruising around the incision – If present, they will seem unsightly within the initial few days after the surgery. However, these marks are nearly always self-limiting and wouldn't leave further scar on the skin.

Mild vaginal bleeding - it should agree a light menstrual period and also the quantity are going to be decreasing within the following days.
