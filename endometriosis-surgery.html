Endometriosis surgeon in Bangalore

The lining tissue of the uterus is named endometrium. Every month a woman produces endometrium among the cavity of the womb. The liner tissues are shed through the cervix into the vagina throughout the menstrual periods.

For unknown reasons, in some women these tissues grow outside the womb. this can be known as endometriosis.

The blood and alternative biochemical's discharged by the endometriosis cells can irritate the surrounding tissues, inflicting pelvic pain. When these cells become active throughout menstrual bleeding, they cause painful periods. Eventually the body could form connective tissue round the injuries caused by endometriosis tissues, inflicting additional severe chronic pain and problem in falling pregnant.

Endometriosis can cause:

Painful periods or chronic pelvic pain that have an effect on normal activities

Fertility problems - even mild degree of endometriosis can cause fertility problems even with natural conception.

Dyspareunia - that it can also cause pain during intercourse effecting the material life of the couple.

Scarring and adhesions around pelvic structures.

Pain or dysfunction in bowel or urinary tract if these organs are affected.

Why surgery?

Surgery is the gold standard to diagnose and treat endometriosis. It is a most effective method to remove endometriosis. A surgery would be helpful to you if:

You have severe pain despite taking painkillers or requiring painkillers for a long duration.

Your pain affects your quality of life and normal activities.

You have a “chocolate cyst” (endometrioma), that is caused by endometriosis within the ovary.

Your fertility specialist have instructed surgery to increase the possibility of natural conception or successful IVF.

You have bowel, bladder or other organ dysfunction that are seemingly to be caused by severe endometriosis.

What are the alternatives to surgery?

Medical treatment is also offered to patients with mild endometriosis or small endometrioma less than 4 cms. It is reasonable to use these medical treatments as first-line therapies to relieve symptoms. But, none of them will remove endometriosis. This symptoms and fertility issues will continue to remain once the women stops using the medication.

The treatments usually consists of:

Simple analgesics - common ones are Paracetamol and other NSAID'S.

Hormone treatments – e.g. Progesterone pill and oral contraceptive pill.

Mirena - a special intrauterine device that slowly unleash a hormone known as levonorgestrel.

Hormonal treatments or Mirena are not appropriate for women who are attempting to get pregnant.

What do you expect after surgery?

Compared to an open surgery, patients who undergone a laparoscopic surgery have less pain and scarring, get back to routine activity earlier and recover faster. Full recovery sometimes takes 2-3 weeks. The recovery time varies depending on the extinct of the endometriosis and the complexity of the surgery.

For women with fertility issues, following surgery they're more likely to conceive naturally.

For patients who continue to have difficulty in conceiving, there are choices of adjuvant medical treatment which will help them to conceive faster. These can include ovulation induction IUI of further treatment in the form of IVF. Our specialists can discuss this with you.
