Laparoscopic Tubal Recanalization Treatment in Bangalore

Laparoscopic Tubal Recanalization is a surgical procedure that restores the patency of blocked fallopian tubes, giving women a renewed chance at natural conception. This minimally invasive technique allows for precise treatment with quicker recovery times compared to traditional surgery. If you're looking for expert care for laparoscopic tubal recanalization treatment in Bangalore, look no further than Dr. Sunil Eshwar. Specializing in fertility treatments, Dr. Sunil Eshwar is renowned for providing successful and minimally invasive solutions for women facing tubal blockages.

Laparoscopic tubal recanalization is a minimally invasive surgical procedure aimed at restoring fertility by reopening previously blocked or ligated fallopian tubes. It is usually performed after a woman who has undergone tubal ligation (a permanent sterilization procedure) decides to attempt to conceive again.

Indications for Surgery

1. Previous tubal ligation: Women who had their tubes tied and now wish to have more children.

2. Tubal blockages: Women with tubal blockage due to infections, inflammation, or conditions like endometriosis, though it is more common after intentional sterilization.

Who Should Perform the Procedure?

Laparoscopic tubal recanalization should be performed by:

Gynaecologic surgeons: Specialized in minimally invasive procedures.

Fertility specialists or reproductive surgeons: These professionals have more experience in handling the delicate structures of the reproductive organs and are well-versed in the complexities of fertility surgery.

How It Is Performed

Laparoscopy: The surgeon makes small incisions in the abdomen and inserts a laparoscope (a thin tube with a camera) to view the pelvic organs.

Reanastomosis: The blocked segments of the fallopian tubes are identified following which any scar tissue or obstruction removed and edges of tubes are freshened open ends of the tubes are meticulously reconnected (reanastomosis) using fine sutures

Assessment: After the procedure, dye is injected through the cervix to check if it flows through the fallopian tubes, ensuring they are open.

Advantages of Surgery

Minimally invasive: Small incisions lead to quicker recovery, less postoperative pain, and minimal scarring.

Restoration of natural fertility: It provides the opportunity for natural conception.

Avoidance of assisted reproduction: If successful, the woman may not need in vitro fertilization (IVF), which can be more expensive and invasive.

Limitations of Surgery

Success rate varies: Success depends on several factors including the location of the blockage, the length of the remaining healthy tube, the woman's age, and her overall fertility health.

Risk of ectopic pregnancy: The procedure increases the risk of a tubal (ectopic) pregnancy, where a fertilized egg implants in the fallopian tube instead of the uterus.

Not suitable for all: It may not be effective in cases of severe tubal damage, such as those caused by extensive scarring or infection.

Surgical risks: As with any surgery, there are risks of bleeding, infection, or injury to surrounding organs.

Alternative options: Some patients may be better suited for IVF, which bypasses the fallopian tubes altogether.

This procedure is typically chosen when patients prefer a more natural path to conception, but it requires careful patient selection for optimal outcomes
